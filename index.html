OFF-BEAT

Short Film — Mixed Media — Montreal, 2025

CONCEPT

OFF-BEAT explores the emotional cost of ambition through the story of a rising Montreal garage band destabilized by sudden success and a mysterious death. As friendships are strained and guilt takes root, the film asks: how far can we chase our dreams before losing ourselves?

FORM

The project adopts a non-linear structure driven by an original song that functions simultaneously as narrative, music video, and emotional backbone. Live-action footage is interwoven with hand-drawn animation, graphic design, and fabricated media elements.

THEMES

Recurring organic motifs such as vines spreading across bodies, instruments, and screens visualize the protagonist's internal collapse. They symbolize growth turning invasive, creativity becoming obsession, and guilt that refuses resolution.

ANIMATION

Animation appears during moments of heightened emotion or memory, representing authenticity and inner truth. These segments serve as poetic interruptions — windows into the characters' subconscious feelings of nostalgia, guilt, and longing.

SYNOPSIS

Set in early-2000s Montreal, OFF-BEAT follows Bloom, a rising grunge garage band. The story unfolds as an apology letter from the singer Sienna Rae to her former drummer Chase Stovsky — a confession shaped by guilt, memory, and the weight of a tragedy that forever changed their band.

Before the tragedy, Bloom experiences its first taste of success. But beneath the noise and applause, tension brews. Sienna makes the painful decision to fire Chase, whose arrogance and self-destructive habits threaten their future. That same night, Chase dies under mysterious circumstances.

Through its fractured timeline, emotional and investigative lens, OFF-BEAT is a raw exploration of how far people will go to chase their dreams and how we can lose ourselves in the process.

SKULL PACK

Illustration — Personal Project — Ongoing

CONCEPT

Skull Pack is an ongoing personal illustration project centered around a recurring skull character. The skulls serve as a signature visual motif — appearing across illustrations, stickers, wallpapers, character designs, and merchandise concepts.

ORIGINS

Born from sketchbook doodles and graffiti-influenced illustration, the skull character became a recurring element across all of Théophile's work — from navigation icons to standalone art pieces.

STYLE

The visual language blends cartoon illustration with street art sensibilities. Clean shapes with glossy highlights, bold outlines, and a playful-yet-dark attitude.

APPLICATIONS

Stickers, phone wallpapers, character designs, clothing mockups, brand identity elements, and web navigation.

NEW YORK

Photography & Typography — 2025/2026

A photographic and typographic exploration of New York City. Shot on Canon EOS R50, 18-45 and DJI Osmo Action 4 during New Year's in New York — a collaboration between Théophile Amiard and Clément Amiard, December 2025 to January 2026.

The series pairs bold, stretched typography with photographs of the Statue of Liberty, creating a layered visual dialogue between the iconic monument and the energy of the city.

DON'T BE AFRAID

Short Film — Animation — 2025

Kaitlyn Pepper & Théophile Amiard

ABOUT

Through animated depictions of medicinal and real-life scenarios, Don't Be Afraid exposes the corruption of prescription medication and its addictive properties. The film challenges government and medical contradictions surrounding drugs and the healthcare systems that promote or suppress specific remedies.

A collaboration between Kaitlyn Pepper and Théophile Amiard, the piece brings awareness to how addictions are mass-produced and the lack of resources to address an opioid crisis initiated by authority.
